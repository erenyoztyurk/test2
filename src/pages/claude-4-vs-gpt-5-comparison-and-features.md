====================================================================

**Architecture**
---------------

Claude 4 and GPT-5 are both large language models (LLMs) developed by Meta and Google, respectively. While both models share some similarities in their architecture, there are key differences that set them apart.

### Claude 4

Claude 4 is built on top of Meta AI's proprietary architecture, which is based on the transformer model. The model consists of multiple transformer layers, each comprising an encoder and a decoder. The encoder takes in input tokens and generates contextualized representations, while the decoder generates output tokens based on these representations.

Claude 4's architecture is designed to handle a wide range of NLP tasks, including language translation, text summarization, and conversational dialogue. The model's robust architecture and training data enable it to achieve state-of-the-art results in many of these tasks.

### GPT-5

GPT-5, on the other hand, is built on top of a more complex architecture that includes multiple transformer layers, self-attention mechanisms, and a novel "memory" module. The memory module allows the model to store and retrieve information from a large knowledge base, enabling it to perform tasks that require a deep understanding of the input data.

GPT-5's architecture is designed to handle a wide range of NLP tasks, including language translation, text summarization, and conversational dialogue. The model's advanced architecture and training data enable it to achieve state-of-the-art results in many of these tasks.

### GPT-5
